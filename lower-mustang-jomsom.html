Overview

A Quick Escape to Hidden Mustang

The Lower Mustang and Jomsom Trek is the perfect introduction to the Mustang region for those with limited time. Starting from the charming town of Jomsom, this 8-day trek ventures through the lower valleys and villages that offer authentic Tibetan culture without the restrictions and high altitude of Upper Mustang.

You'll trek along the extraordinary Kali Gandaki Gorge, one of the world's deepest river canyons, visiting medieval villages like Kagbeni and Marpha with their traditional architecture, whitewashed houses, and vibrant culture. The region is also famous for its apple orchards and unique local wines.

This trek is ideal for travelers seeking cultural immersion, stunning mountain scenery, and authentic village experiences, all without extreme altitude or technical challenges. Jomsom itself sits at 2,720m and is the gateway to Mustang, offering spectacular views of Dhaulagiri and Nilgiri peaks.

The Route

8 Days Itinerary

01
Kathmandu to Pokhara

Scenic drive from Kathmandu to the lakeside city of Pokhara (approx. 6-7 hours). Afternoon at leisure exploring the Phewa Lake area or visit the Shanti Stupa for panoramic views.

02
Pokhara Flight to Jomsom

Early morning scenic flight from Pokhara to Jomsom (30 mins), flying between the Dhaulagiri and Annapurna ranges. One of the most spectacular mountain flights in the world! Afternoon in Jomsom exploring the town and acclimatizing. (Alt: 2,720m)

03
Jomsom to Marpha

Easy trek along the Kali Gandaki riverbed to the beautiful village of Marpha. Visit the Marpha monasteries and explore the narrow bazaar. Evening: sample local apple brandy and wine. (Alt: 2,670m, Walking: 4–5 hrs)

04
Marpha to Tukuche

Trek through apple orchards and past traditional whitewashed houses to Tukuche, a former trading post. Visit the ancient gompa and enjoy views of Tilicho Peak. (Alt: 2,590m, Walking: 4–5 hrs)

05
Tukuche to Kagbeni

Trek uphill through the scenic Kali Gandaki gorge. Kagbeni is a medieval fortress village at the entrance to Upper Mustang. Explore the ancient stone houses, monasteries, and the walled settlement. (Alt: 2,810m, Walking: 5–6 hrs)

06
Kagbeni Acclimatization & Exploration

Full day in Kagbeni for acclimatization and exploration. Hike to nearby viewpoints for views of Nilgiri and Dhaulagiri. Visit local monasteries and chai houses. Explore the junction where the Kali Gandaki and Jhomsom rivers meet. (Alt: 2,810m)

07
Kagbeni to Lete

Trek downhill through the Kali Gandaki gorge, passing through scenic landscape with towering cliffs. Lete is a quiet village surrounded by apple orchards. Relax and enjoy local hospitality. (Alt: 2,480m, Walking: 5 hrs)

08
Lete to Jomsom & Return to Pokhara

Easy trek back to Jomsom (2 hours). Afternoon flight back to Pokhara. Evening drive towards Kathmandu or stay in Pokhara for additional exploration. Farewell dinner.

Common Questions

Lower Mustang & Jomsom FAQs

Quick answers for your journey through the Kali Gandaki valley

How difficult is the Lower Mustang & Jomsom trek?

This is considered an easy to moderate trek. The maximum altitude is relatively low (2,950m at Muktinath/Kagbeni), and the trails are well-established. It's an excellent choice for first-time trekkers or those who want to experience Mustang's culture without extreme physical challenge.

What is the best time for this trek?

Unlike many other treks, the Jomsom area is in a rain shadow, making it a great option even during the Monsoon (June-August). However, the most popular times are Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) for the clearest mountain views.

Do I need a special permit for Lower Mustang?

No, you only need the standard ACAP (Annapurna Conservation Area Permit) and a TIMS card. The expensive $500 Restricted Area Permit is only required for Upper Mustang (beyond Kagbeni).

How do I get to Jomsom?

The most common way is a scenic 20-minute flight from Pokhara. You can also reach Jomsom by a long and bumpy 10-12 hour jeep drive from Pokhara, which is an adventure in itself as you drive through the deepest gorge in the world.

Is the water safe to drink?

You should not drink tap water directly. We recommend using water purification tablets or buying boiled water at teahouses. Marpha and other villages also have "Safe Drinking Water" stations where you can refill your bottles for a small fee.
