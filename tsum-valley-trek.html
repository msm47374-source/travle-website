Overview

The Valley of Sacred Happiness

The Tsum Valley is a sacred Himalayan pilgrimage valley situated in northern Gorkha, Nepal. 'Tsum' is derived from the Tibetan word 'Tsombo', which means vivid. Traditionally, the valley was a distinct geographical area called Tsum Tso Chuksum.

Against the backdrop of the majestic Ganesh Himal, Sringi Himal, and Boudha Himal ranges, this hidden valley is rich in ancient Buddhist culture. The people of Tsum Valley, known as Tsumbas, are of Tibetan origin and have their own unique dialect and traditions. The valley is known for its strict practice of non-violence (Shyakya), where no animals are slaughtered.

Restricted Area Trek

Tsum Valley is a restricted area. Trekkers require a special Restricted Area Permit, and it is mandatory to trek with a licensed guide. All logistics and permits are handled by HimalayaQuest.

Highlights

Sacred Wonders

  • Mu Gompa

    The largest and oldest monastery in the valley, perched at 3,700m with stunning mountain vistas.

  • Piren Phu Cave

    The sacred cave where the great Buddhist saint Milarepa is said to have meditated.

  • Ganesh Himal Views

    Unrivaled views of the Ganesh Himal range, Sringi Himal, and Boudha Himal.

  • Hidden Culture

    Authentic Tibetan-influenced lifestyle and the unique 'Shyakya' tradition of non-violence.

Common Questions

Tsum Valley FAQs

Essential information for your journey into the sacred hidden valley

What makes Tsum Valley different from other treks?

Tsum Valley is unique for its deep spiritual atmosphere and strict adherence to non-violence (Shyakya). No animals are slaughtered in the valley, and the culture is purely Tibetan Buddhist. It feels like stepping back in time to an ancient, untouched Himalayan sanctuary.

Do I need a special permit for Tsum Valley?

Yes, Tsum Valley is a restricted area. You need a special Restricted Area Permit (RAP), which costs $40 for the first week during Autumn and $30 during other seasons. You must also trek with a licensed guide and be in a group of at least two people.

How difficult is the Tsum Valley trek?

It is considered a moderate trek. While it involves several days of 5-7 hour walks and some steep sections, the maximum altitude at Mu Gompa (3,700m) is lower than many other treks, making the risk of altitude sickness much lower.

Can I combine Tsum Valley with the Manaslu Circuit?

Yes, many trekkers combine the two for an epic 22-25 day journey. You first explore the sacred Tsum Valley and then rejoin the main Manaslu Circuit trail at Lokpa to continue toward the Larkya La Pass.

What is the accommodation like in Tsum Valley?

Accommodation is in simple teahouses and community homestays. They are more basic than those on the Everest or Annapurna trails, but they offer a very authentic experience. You'll enjoy local Tibetan-style food and warm Himalayan hospitality.
