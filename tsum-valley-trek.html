Overview

The Valley of Sacred Happiness

The Tsum Valley is a sacred Himalayan pilgrimage valley situated in northern Gorkha, Nepal. 'Tsum' is derived from the Tibetan word 'Tsombo', which means vivid. Traditionally, the valley was a distinct geographical area called Tsum Tso Chuksum.

Against the backdrop of the majestic Ganesh Himal, Sringi Himal, and Boudha Himal ranges, this hidden valley is rich in ancient Buddhist culture. The people of Tsum Valley, known as Tsumbas, are of Tibetan origin and have their own unique dialect and traditions. The valley is known for its strict practice of non-violence (Shyakya), where no animals are slaughtered.

Restricted Area Trek

Tsum Valley is a restricted area. Trekkers require a special Restricted Area Permit, and it is mandatory to trek with a licensed guide. All logistics and permits are handled by BESTTREKSNEPAL.

Highlights

Sacred Wonders

  • Mu Gompa

    The largest and oldest monastery in the valley, perched at 3,700m with stunning mountain vistas.

  • Piren Phu Cave

    The sacred cave where the great Buddhist saint Milarepa is said to have meditated.

  • Ganesh Himal Views

    Unrivaled views of the Ganesh Himal range, Sringi Himal, and Boudha Himal.

  • Hidden Culture

    Authentic Tibetan-influenced lifestyle and the unique 'Shyakya' tradition of non-violence.

  • Rachen Gompa

    A significant nunnery located in the Shiar Khola valley, rich in murals and statues.

  • Shiar Khola River

    Trek alongside the pristine glacial waters that flow through the heart of the valley.

Common Questions

Tsum Valley FAQs

What makes Tsum Valley different?
Tsum Valley is unique for its deep spiritual atmosphere and strict adherence to non-violence (Shyakya). No animals are slaughtered, and the culture is purely Tibetan Buddhist.
Do I need a special permit?
Yes, Tsum Valley is a restricted area. You need a Restricted Area Permit (RAP), must trek with a licensed guide, and be in a group of at least two people.
How difficult is the trek?
It is a moderate trek. Max altitude is 3,700m at Mu Gompa, making it safer regarding altitude sickness compared to Everest or Annapurna circuits.
