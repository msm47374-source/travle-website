Regional Overview

The Hidden Wilderness

Dolpa is Nepal's largest and most remote district, home to the Shey Phoksundo National Park and cultures that have remained unchanged for centuries.

Phoksundo Lake

Witness the mesmerizing deep blue-green waters of Nepal's deepest alpine lake, a true jewel of the Himalayas.

Shey Gompa

Visit the 800-year-old monastery at the base of the Crystal Mountain, a sacred site for Buddhist pilgrims.

Land of Snow Leopards

The region is one of the most vital habitats for the elusive Snow Leopard, as well as Blue Sheep and Himalayan Tahr.

Nature & Geography

The Mystical Plateau

Upper Dolpa is part of the Tibetan Plateau, characterized by arid landscapes and high mountain passes. The region remains largely untouched by modern infrastructure.

  • National Park: Shey Phoksundo is Nepal's largest national park (3,555 sq km).
  • Bon Culture: One of the few places where the pre-Buddhist Bon religion is still practiced.
  • Remote Access: Requires a flight to Juphal followed by days of trekking through wild terrain.

Region Guide

Dolpa Region FAQs

Essential information for your journey into Nepal's wildest frontier

What is the difference between Upper and Lower Dolpa?

Lower Dolpa focuses on the stunning Phoksundo Lake and is a moderate trek. Upper Dolpa is a much longer, more remote expedition that crosses several high passes and reaches the sacred Shey Gompa. Upper Dolpa requires a more expensive restricted area permit ($500 for 10 days).

How do I get to the Dolpa region?

The journey starts with a flight from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj, followed by a dramatic early morning flight into the mountain airstrip at Juphal. These flights are highly weather-dependent, so we recommend building in a few extra days to your itinerary.

What is the best time to trek in Dolpa?

Unlike most of Nepal, Dolpa is a great destination for Summer/Monsoon trekking (June-September). Much of the region lies in a rain shadow, keeping it dry while the rest of the country is wet. Spring and Autumn are also excellent, but winter is too cold and passes are often blocked by snow.

Is it true that Bon culture is still practiced here?

Yes, Dolpa is one of the last strongholds of the pre-Buddhist Bon religion. You'll see Bon monasteries (where you walk counter-clockwise) alongside Tibetan Buddhist sites. The culture here is incredibly ancient and has remained largely unchanged for centuries.

How difficult are the treks in Dolpa?

Treks in Dolpa are considered challenging to difficult. The terrain is rugged, the trails are remote, and you'll be crossing multiple passes above 5,000m. Excellent physical fitness and previous high-altitude trekking experience are strongly recommended for Upper Dolpa expeditions.
