Overview

An Elite Challenge

The North Ridge Trek is an exclusive 20-day alpine expedition for experienced trekkers and mountaineers. This challenging route circumnavigates Makalu via the technical north ridge, offering steep terrain, high altitude passes, and unparalleled views of the fifth-highest mountain and its surrounding giants.

This trek demands physical fitness, mountaineering experience, and mental resilience. Rewards include pristine wilderness rarely seen by tourists, technical climbing sections, and intimate encounters with mountain culture in isolated communities deep in the Makalu region.

Day-by-Day

Detailed Itinerary

01 Arrival in Kathmandu

Welcome to Nepal! Meet our team for trek briefing and equipment check. Prepare for one of the world's most challenging alpine expeditions.

02 Acclimatization & Gear Preparation

Explore Kathmandu heritage sites and conduct a detailed equipment check with your climbing team. Final altitude acclimatization day in the city.

03 Drive to Tumlingtar

Travel through the scenic Arun Valley to Tumlingtar. Meet your porters and finalize logistics for the expedition.

04 Trek to Khandbari

Begin the expedition with a 5-hour trek through subtropical forests along the Arun River to Khandbari (1,440m).

05 Khandbari to Rampur

Continue through verdant hills to Rampur (2,260m). Mountain views increase as we gain elevation.

06 Rampur to Num

Trek through rhododendron forests ascending to Num (2,580m). First views of major alpine peaks.

07 Num to Seduwa via Northern Pass

Technical hiking across alpine terrain. First introduction to steeper gradients and altitude effects.

08 Seduwa to Salpa Pokhari

Trek to pristine alpine lake at 3,200m surrounded by high peaks and glaciers. Magnificent views in all directions.

09 Salpa Pokhari to North Tashigaon

Trek the northern route toward Makalu along the remote northern valleys. Altitude gain to 3,100m.

10 North Tashigaon to North Ridge Base

Reach the North Ridge approach camp (3,600m). Introduction to technical terrain begins. Equipment check and rope work practice.

11 Technical Training & Acclimatization

Intensive climbing training day including rope skills, ice climbing, and rock scrambling with professional mountain guides.

12 High Camp Ridge Traverse

Technical rock and ice climbing to High Camp (5,200m). Fixed ropes on steep sections. Stunning alpine vistas emerge.

13 High Camp Summit Push

Push to North Ridge High Point (5,600m). Master technical climbing on steep ice and rock. Breathtaking 360-degree panoramas.

14 Traverse to East Ridge

Descend and traverse toward the east ridge with continued technical sections. Descent to intermediate camp (4,800m).

15 East Ridge Camp & Descent

Trek from east ridge camp back toward lower elevations. Trail offers final iconic views of Makalu from new perspectives.

16 Return to Seduwa

Continue descent through alpine meadows back to Seduwa (3,000m). Reflect on the expedition's achievements.

17 Descent to Tumlingtar

Final trek days as we descend to Tumlingtar (starting point). Celebrate with local community and guides.

18 Drive back to Kathmandu

Long drive back to Kathmandu. Rest and recovery after the intense expedition.

19 Rest & Celebration Day

Final celebration dinner with the team. Collect expedition photographs and finalize records of your achievement.

20 Departure

Transfer to airport for departure. Safe travels home with memories of an elite alpine adventure.

Help Center

Common Questions

What experience do I need for this trek?

This is for experienced mountaineers with prior high-altitude and technical climbing experience. You must be comfortable with rock climbing (Grade II), ice climbing, and working with ropes at altitude. Prior successful summits of 6,000m peaks are strongly recommended.

What are the physical requirements?

Outstanding fitness is essential. You should be prepared for 6-8 hours of daily hiking at altitude with significant elevation gain, technical climbing sections, and intense physical exertion. Pre-trek training including altitude running, rock climbing practice, and ice climbing lessons are highly recommended.

How is altitude sickness managed?

We incorporate multiple acclimatization days and employ experienced guides trained in altitude sickness recognition. Gradual altitude gain schedule, proper hydration, and medical support are standard. In case of severe symptoms, we can rapidly descend to lower altitudes and access helicopter evacuation if needed.

Is technical climbing training provided?

Yes, we provide intensive climbing training during the expedition with expert guides. This includes rope work, ice climbing technique, rock scrambling, and altitude-specific skills. However, basic climbing competency before arrival is essential and expected.

What's included in the climbing equipment?

We provide: ropes, harnesses, ice axes, crampons, carabiners, belay devices, and safety gear. You should bring personal climbing shoes, helmet, and insulated gloves. We arrange an optional pre-trek rental of technical gear at competitive rates if you prefer not to transport your own equipment.
