Overview

A Journey Through Time and Sand

Upper Mustang is Nepal's legendary "Last Forbidden Kingdom", an arid Himalayan region where Tibetan Buddhist heritage remains deeply preserved.

Unlike forested trekking regions, Mustang offers dramatic red cliffs, deep canyons, and open plateaus in the rain shadow of the Himalayas, making it ideal even during parts of the monsoon period.

The cultural heart of the route is Lo Manthang, where ancient monasteries, cave dwellings, and walled settlements create one of the most distinct trekking experiences in Nepal.

Common Questions

Upper Mustang FAQ

How difficult is the Upper Mustang Trek?
This route is moderate overall. Altitude and wind exposure are the main factors, while daily walking distances are generally manageable.
Why is the permit cost higher than other regions?
Upper Mustang is a restricted area. Permit regulations help preserve its fragile environment and unique cultural heritage.
Can I trek Upper Mustang independently?
No. You must travel through a registered agency with a licensed guide and valid restricted-area permits.
