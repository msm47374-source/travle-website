The Last Forbidden Kingdom

Upper Mustang Trek

Step into the hidden world of Lo Manthang, where ancient Tibetan culture meets a surreal desert landscape.

Duration 14 Days

Difficulty Moderate

Best Season Mar–Nov (Inc. Monsoon)
Overview

A Journey Through Time and Sand

Upper Mustang is one of the most mysterious and beautiful regions in Nepal. Often referred to as the "Last Forbidden Kingdom", this high-altitude desert was closed to foreigners until 1992. Today, it remains a strictly regulated area, preserving an authentic Tibetan culture that has barely changed in centuries.

Unlike the lush valleys of the Annapurna or the jagged ice of Everest, Mustang offers a landscape of eroded red cliffs, deep canyons, and windswept plateaus. Being in the rain shadow of the Himalayas, it is one of the few treks in Nepal that can be comfortably enjoyed even during the summer monsoon season.

The highlight is the walled city of Lo Manthang, the capital of the former Kingdom of Lo. Here, you'll walk through narrow alleys, visit 14th-century monasteries filled with exquisite murals, and perhaps even meet members of the local royal lineage.

Highlights

Why You'll Be Captivated

  • Lo Manthang Walled City

    Explore the ancient capital of the Mustang kingdom, founded in 1380.

  • Red Cliffs & Arid Scenery

    Marvel at the wind-sculpted rock formations and colorful deep canyons.

  • Sky Caves of Chhoser

    Discover thousands of ancient caves carved into vertical cliff walls.

  • 14th-Century Monasteries

    Visit Thubchen and Jampa Lhakhang with their vibrant wall paintings.

  • Kali Gandaki Gorge

    Trek along the upper reaches of one of the world's deepest river gorges.

  • Pure Tibetan Culture

    Experience the unique traditions, language, and lifestyle of the Loba people.

The Route

14 Days Itinerary

01
Arrival & Drive to Pokhara

Scenic drive from Kathmandu to the lakeside city of Pokhara. Evening at leisure by the lake.

02
Flight to Jomsom & Trek to Kagbeni

Early flight between Dhaulagiri and Annapurna ranges to Jomsom. Trek along the Kali Gandaki river to the medieval village of Kagbeni. (Alt: 2,810m)

03
Kagbeni to Chele

Enter the restricted area of Upper Mustang. The trail climbs through Tangbe village to the red cliffs of Chele. (Alt: 3,050m)

04
Chele to Syangboche

Cross several passes with panoramic views of Tilicho and Nilgiri peaks. Pass through traditional Loba villages. (Alt: 3,800m)

05
Syangboche to Ghami

Ascend to the Yamada La pass (3,850m) and then the Nyi La pass (4,010m). Descend into the beautiful village of Ghami, surrounded by desert greenery. (Alt: 3,520m)

06
Ghami to Tsarang

Pass the longest Mani wall in Mustang, then climb to the Tsarang La pass. Arrive in Tsarang, home to a massive 14th-century fortress and red gompa. (Alt: 3,500m)

07
Arrival at Lo Manthang

The trail descends gently into the plain of aspiration, revealing the majestic walled city of Lo Manthang. (Alt: 3,840m)

08
Exploration Day at Lo Manthang

Visit Jampa Lhakhang, Thubchen Gompa, and the Royal Palace. Optional ride to the Chhoser cave complex.

09
Lo Manthang to Dhakmar

Trek via the ancient Ghar Gompa, one of the oldest monasteries in Mustang. Continue through dramatic red rock landscapes to the village of Dhakmar. (Alt: 3,820m)

10
Dhakmar to Ghiling

A scenic walk through high passes and traditional villages. The trail offers fantastic views of the Dhaulagiri and Nilgiri massifs during the descent to Ghiling. (Alt: 3,570m)

11
Ghiling to Chhusang

Retrace your steps back towards the lower reaches of Mustang. Enjoy the changing colors of the canyon walls as you descend to the village of Chhusang. (Alt: 2,980m)

12
Chhusang to Jomsom

Final day of trekking back to Jomsom. After exiting the restricted area at Kagbeni, enjoy a relaxed walk along the Kali Gandaki riverbed. (Alt: 2,720m)

13
Flight Jomsom to Pokhara

Early morning flight back to Pokhara. Spend the rest of the day relaxing by the Phewa Lake or exploring the Peace Pagoda.

14
Return to Kathmandu

Final drive from Pokhara back to Kathmandu. Farewell dinner.

Trip Details

Upper Mustang FAQs

Why is the Upper Mustang permit so expensive?
Upper Mustang is a restricted area controlled by the Department of Immigration to preserve its fragile culture and environment. The $500 permit (for 10 days) acts as a high-value, low-impact tourism strategy.
Can I trek Upper Mustang during the monsoon?
Yes! This is one of the best treks for summer (June-August). Mustang lies in the rain shadow of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges, meaning it receives very little rain while the rest of Nepal is in peak monsoon.
How difficult is the trek compared to EBC?
Upper Mustang is generally easier than Everest Base Camp. The maximum altitude is lower (around 3,800m-4,000m) and the trails are less steep, though the wind and arid climate can be challenging.
Are there ATMs in Lo Manthang?
While there are now a few ATMs in Jomsom and even Lo Manthang, they are often unreliable or out of cash. It is highly recommended to carry enough Nepalese Rupees from Pokhara or Kathmandu.
