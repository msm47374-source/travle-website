Overview

The Sacred Alpine Sanctuary

Click any day to expand the detailed route information

The Gosainkunda Lake Trek is one of Nepal's most revered spiritual and natural journeys. Located within the Langtang National Park, this trek takes you to the holy Gosainkunda Lake (4,380m), a site of immense religious significance for both Hindus and Buddhists. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva created the lake by striking his trident into the mountain to quench his thirst.

The trail begins with a drive to Dhunche and ascends through lush rhododendron and oak forests, home to the elusive Red Panda. As you climb higher, the landscape transitions into rugged alpine terrain, offering spectacular panoramas of the Ganesh Himal, Langtang Lirung, and the Manaslu range.

The highlight of the trek is the crystal-clear waters of the sacred lake itself, often surrounded by several other smaller high-altitude lakes like Bhairab Kunda and Saraswati Kunda. Whether you seek spiritual peace or high-altitude adventure, Gosainkunda delivers an unforgettable Himalayan experience.

Daily Route

Detailed Itinerary

Click any day to expand the detailed route information

01 Arrival in Kathmandu

Welcome to Nepal! Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met by our representative and transferred to your hotel. Evening briefing about the trek and a welcome dinner. Overnight in Kathmandu.

02 Drive to Dhunche

A scenic drive of 7-8 hours takes us through the winding roads of hills and valleys to Dhunche, the administrative headquarters of the Rasuwa district. The journey offers glimpses of local life and terraced hillsides. Overnight in Dhunche (1,960m).

03 Trek to Shin Gompa

The trek begins with a steady ascent through forests of oak, pine, and rhododendron. We pass through Deurali (2,625m) before reaching the peaceful village of Shin Gompa (3,330m). Visit the local cheese factory and the old monastery.

04 Reach Gosainkunda Lake

A more challenging day with significant altitude gain. The ascent leads to Lauribinayak (3,910m), offering stunning views of the Ganesh Himal and Langtang Lirung. Continuing upward, we reach the sacred Gosainkunda Lake (4,380m).

05 Explore & Return to Shin Gompa

Early morning exploration of the lake and its surroundings. You can hike to nearby viewpoints for sunrise. After breakfast, we begin our descent back toward Shin Gompa (3,330m), retracing our steps with magnificent mountain views.

06 Trek back to Dhunche

We continue our descent through the lush forests down to Dhunche (1,960m). It's a pleasant walk as the air becomes warmer and richer in oxygen. Enjoy a farewell dinner with the trekking crew.

07 Return to Kathmandu

After breakfast, we board our vehicle for the drive back to Kathmandu. Upon arrival, you'll be transferred to your hotel. The rest of the day is free for shopping or relaxation in Thamel. Overnight in Kathmandu.

08 Final Departure

Our representative will transfer you to the international airport for your flight back home or to your next destination. Namaste!

Common Questions

Gosainkunda Lake FAQ

Click any day to expand the detailed route information

What is the religious significance of Gosainkunda?
Gosainkunda is a sacred pilgrimage site for both Hindus and Buddhists. Hindus believe the lake was created by Lord Shiva's trident. Thousands of pilgrims visit during the Janai Purnima festival in August to take a holy dip in its waters, which are believed to wash away sins.
How difficult is the trek to Gosainkunda?
The trek is considered moderate. The main challenge is the rapid altitude gain from Dhunche (1,960m) to the lakes (4,380m). While the trails are well-defined, the steep ascents require good cardiovascular fitness and careful acclimatization.
Can I see other lakes besides Gosainkunda?
Yes! There are over 100 lakes in the Gosainkunda region. The most prominent ones near the main trail are Bhairab Kunda and Saraswati Kunda. Each has its own spiritual significance and stunning turquoise beauty.
What is the best time for this trek?
Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) are the best seasons. In spring, the rhododendrons are in full bloom. Autumn offers the clearest views of the Ganesh Himal and Langtang Lirung. Winter trekking is possible but the lakes may be frozen and the passes blocked by snow.
Is it possible to cross the Lauribina Pass?
Yes, many trekkers continue from the lakes over the Lauribina Pass (4,610m) to reach the Helambu region. This adds a challenging pass crossing to the itinerary but rewards you with some of the best panoramic views in the entire Langtang region.
